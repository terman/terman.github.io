Canyons! — Summer 2016

Zion owes its existence to the Virgin River, which you see here emerging from the Narrows into the wider lower canyon. We're there bright and early, with the east light creating a marvelous lightscape on the western canyon walls.
You can actually hike quite far up into the Narrows. Well, hike isn't quite the right word: it's more splashing, wading, or swimming in cold water. Next time we'll rent the right shoes and give it a try!j
A 40-minute ride using the Park's convenient shuttle system takes you from the lush riparian environment in the north...
through the former canyon-bottom farmlands of the Mormons in the middle...
to the arid high-dessert landscape at the southern end of the Park.
