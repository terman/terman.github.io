26 August 2016

Some hikes have little surprises waiting for you. Today's hike included this 200' long suspension bridge over a deep ravine. This one was particularly springy and the crosswind blowing up the ravine added a bit of sway, which made for an interesting 5 minutes.

But the bridge was just the warm up for the individual narrow metal steps down the 300' cliff face. Reminded me of the Nintendo video game where you have to get Mario from floating platform to floating platform, but without the extra lives. Maybe next time I'll take a moment to translate the warning sign at the trailhead :)

23 August 2016

Actually, more like Berghaus (mountain inn) of the day. The Swiss are good at tucking the se inns into scenic corners along the popular walking routes. They vary from mountain huts with very simple fare to full-blown restaurants like this one. In all cases the cheapest drink on the menu is beer -- the Swiss are clear on their priorities. The remote huts are supplied by helicopter. This Berghaus has its own little cable car for bringing up supplies from a cog railway station far below. Open inns fly a Swiss flag -- always a welcome sight when you're starting to feel peckish!

20 August 2016

On the slopes of Mt. Rigi looking down on the clouds and Lake Lucern. Many opportunities on today's tromp through the high meadows to commune with contented cows, who said to say "hi". Or maybe it was "remember to clean your shoes."

12 August 2016

Many times we gain access to the high meadows via trails that climb up the steep valleys cut by the streams that carry the snow melt and runoff from the glaciers. Here we're walking down from Trift, a mountain hotel that services ice climbers tackling the glaciers we passed earlier in the day. You can just make out the Gornergrat on the top of the distant ridge, a tourist destination that overlooks a spectacular collection of glaciers on the far side. Sadly, glaciers are a sight that might be gone in a couple of generations.

11 August 2016

And here's the most famous alp view of all: the Matterhorn, dominating the skyline above Zermatt, which you can see in the valley below. Actually the word "alp" refers to the high mountain meadows where the cows spend the summer grazing. Not many cows in the Matter valley though -- probably crowded out by the tourists :). But a hiking trail headed up quickly thins the crowd to the intrepid few.

10 August 2015

This is the view from our balcony looking toward the Breithorn, the snow-covered peak in the distance. The hotel is high above tourist-filled Zermatt at the first stop of a gondola system that reaches up to the winter ski areas nestled under the Matterhorn. The cool mountain air is a pleasant relief from the muggy lowlands we passed through today as we traveled to Zermatt.

9 August 2015

View down into the Lauterbrunnen valley as I leave Wengen on the train, headed to Zurich to meet Debbie. This is a classic U-shaped valley carved by glaciers. The sides are sheer cliffs up to 2700' high, perfect for BASE jumping or paragliding. Time to order my wing suit!

8 August 2015

Wengen, the town where we're staying, is visible on the plateau across the valley. The snow-covered peaks beyond are "the top of Europe": left to right in the center are the Eiger, Monch, and Jungfrau.  We've hiked up to the Lobhornhutte, which wins the award for the mountain hut with the best view. Note the Nepalese prayer flags on the right -- they were collecting donations for earthquake relief in Nepal, one mountain community to another.
